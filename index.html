Frequently Asked Questions

What is structured regulatory data?

Structured regulatory data is the process of converting unstructured legal and regulatory text into machine-readable engineering requirements. Certivity automates the extraction, structuring, and validation of these requirements so teams can use them directly in their workflows without manually interpreting PDFs.

How does Certivity differ from traditional compliance tools?

Traditional tools focus on tracking regulatory updates. Certivity goes further by transforming regulatory text into actionable engineering requirements, mapping them to product architectures, and synchronizing updates across engineering tools such as DOORS, Jama, Codebeamer, and Polarion.

Which automotive standards does Certivity support?

Certivity supports global automotive frameworks including ISO 26262, ISO/SAE 21434, UNECE WP.29, REACH, ELV, RoHS, PFAS restrictions, and additional standards depending on region and product scope.

Does Certivity replace requirements management tools?

No. Certivity complements existing engineering environments by feeding validated, structured regulatory requirements into your requirements management tool. It ensures the information in your workflow stays accurate, current, and compliant.

How often are regulations updated?

Certivity continuously monitors regulatory sources across 50+ jurisdictions. When a change is detected, related requirements are updated and versioned automatically, providing real-time visibility into what changed and why.

Who benefits from using Certivity?

Certivity is designed for engineering-driven organizations developing safety-critical or regulated products. Teams in functional safety, cybersecurity, systems engineering, and compliance benefit from improved accuracy, visibility, and reduced rework.

Do I need to change my engineering processes?

No. Certivity adapts to your current workflows and engineering tools, minimizing the need for operational changes while increasing regulatory accuracy.

Can Certivity support multiple product lines and regions?

Yes. Certivity maps regulatory requirements to multiple product architectures and maintains jurisdiction-specific versions, ensuring compliance across markets and platforms.
